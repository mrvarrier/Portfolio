01. CONTEXT

The Remote Communication Crisis

COVID-19 transformed how we work and communicate, but video calls created an emotional disconnect that affects hiring, marketing, and team collaboration.

The Problem We Discovered

Remote work became the norm, but humans weren't designed to connect emotionally through screens. This shift created a major emotional gap that affects:

• HR Professionals: Struggle to read candidates during virtual interviews

• Marketing Teams: Can't gauge audience reactions in virtual presentations

• Managers: Miss emotional cues that indicate team engagement and morale

🎯

Target Audience

HR professionals and marketing teams who need to understand emotional responses during virtual interactions.

⏱️

Time Constraint

36-hour hackathon sprint requiring rapid research, design, development, and validation.

👥

Team Collaboration

Multidisciplinary team with UX designer (me), two developers, and one business analyst.

02. RESEARCH

Understanding User Needs Under Pressure

Despite the 36-hour constraint, we conducted targeted research to validate our problem and identify the most valuable solution approach.

Conducted desk research
Our business analyst and I researched why people prefer remote jobs and why certain video platforms succeed while others fail. This helped us identify opportunities for our emotional analytics solution.
Talked with users of popular video calling platforms
I spoke with regular Google Meet and Zoom users to understand their experiences. Since these platforms are already widely adopted, we wanted to build something that wouldn't force users to change their established workflows.
Interviewed potential users across different roles
I interviewed people with various job titles from different companies to pinpoint exactly who would benefit most from emotional analytics during video calls.
INSIGHT 01
Users preferred Zoom's rich features but Google Meet's simplicity. They wanted quick access to functionality without interface clutter. This informed our design approach of powerful analytics with clean, intuitive presentation.
INSIGHT 02
HR professionals and marketing teams emerged as our primary target audience, showing the strongest need for emotional analytics during remote interactions to improve hiring decisions and presentation effectiveness.
INSIGHT 03
The core problem crystallized: hiring managers and marketing teams struggle to read emotional cues during video conferences, creating a significant disconnect that affects relationship building and decision-making.

Problem Statement

"How might we help marketing and HR professionals better understand and respond to the emotional dynamics of their virtual meetings?"

03. SOLUTION

Empath.ly: Real-Time Emotional Analytics

A video conferencing platform that analyzes emotions 60 times per second and provides actionable insights through an intuitive dashboard.

Core Innovation

Real-time emotion detection: Our ML model analyzes facial expressions 60 times per second during video calls.

Comprehensive dashboard: Post-meeting analytics show emotional patterns, peaks, and insights tied to specific moments.

Actionable insights: Transcription highlights linked to emotional spikes help users understand what drove reactions.

04. DESIGN PROCESS

Designing for Usability Under Pressure

Designing for Usability

My first priority was creating a user-friendly landing page. Our research showed that users struggle with cluttered interfaces on platforms like Zoom, wasting valuable time for busy professionals.

I designed clear pathways to start or join meetings without distractions, focusing on efficiency over feature exposure.

Leveraging Familiar Design Patterns

Since users are already accustomed to Google Meet and Zoom, I recognized the importance of working with established mental models rather than forcing them to learn entirely new systems.

I designed our video interface with familiar elements inspired by Google Meet, reducing the learning curve and helping users feel comfortable immediately.

Biggest Challenge: Effective Data Visualization

The most significant design challenge was presenting emotional data in a meaningful and accessible way. We needed visualizations that anyone could understand at a glance, regardless of their data literacy.

My team and I collaborated extensively, exploring various data representation methods to find the perfect balance between comprehensive insights and clarity.

Key Learnings

Context-driven design: Deepened understanding of how different user contexts impact design decisions and feature prioritization.

Pressure performance: Learned to prioritize effectively and deliver high-quality work under extreme time constraints.

Integration over replacement: Discovered that users prefer enhancements to existing tools rather than completely new platforms.

Dashboard Design Evolution

The final dashboard was designed to meet diverse user needs with three distinct data presentation methods: quick pie chart overview for busy professionals, detailed timeline graphs for in-depth analysis, and transcript highlights linked to emotional peaks.

This multi-layered approach ensures users can access the level of detail they need without being overwhelmed by unnecessary complexity.
