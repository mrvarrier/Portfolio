Empath.ly

Real-time emotion analytics for video conferencing platforms, bridging the emotional disconnect in remote communication through innovative machine learning and user-centered design.

01. OVERVIEW

Emotion-Aware Video Conferencing

Empath.ly is an innovative video conferencing platform with integrated emotional analytics and real-time emotion identification. During video calls, our system analyzes participants' emotions sixty times per second through their camera feed. After each call, users can access a comprehensive dashboard displaying emotional data and actionable insights, including predominant emotions throughout the meeting, emotional patterns over time, and notable shifts or contrasts in participants' emotional states.

03. CONTEXT & CHALLENGE

The Emotional Disconnect Crisis

COVID-19 fundamentally changed how we communicate and work by making remote interactions the new normal. However, this shift created a major emotional gap—humans weren't designed to connect emotionally through screens. As video calls become standard, marketers and managers struggle with the missing emotional cues and engagement that come naturally in person. People with sensory challenges face even greater barriers. This situation calls for better tools to understand the feelings expressed during online meetings.
04. RESEARCH & DISCOVERY

Understanding Users Under Pressure

Conducted desk research
Our business analyst and I researched to understand both the problem and the market better. We investigated why people prefer remote jobs and why certain video platforms succeed while others fail. This helped us identify opportunities for our solution.
Talked with users of popular video calling platforms
I spoke with people who regularly use Google Meet and Zoom to understand their experiences. Since these platforms are already widely used, we wanted to build something that wouldn't force users to change their habits. These conversations during the hackathon gave us valuable insights into user expectations.
Interviewed potential users across different roles
I interviewed people with various job titles from different companies to pinpoint exactly who would benefit most from our solution. These conversations helped us define our target audience more clearly.
INSIGHT 01
Our research revealed the need for balance between features and usability. When speaking with users of Google Meet and Zoom, we found they preferred Zoom's richer feature set but Google Meet's simpler flow. Users appreciated how Google Meet let them quickly join and use the platform for their intended purpose.
INSIGHT 02
Through interviews with professionals across various roles and companies, we identified our primary target audience: HR professionals and marketing teams. These groups showed the strongest need for emotional analytics during remote interactions.
INSIGHT 03
Our breakthrough "Aha moment" came when we pinpointed the core problem: hiring managers and marketing teams struggle to read emotional cues during video conferences. This creates a significant emotional disconnect that affects their ability to build relationships and make informed decisions.

Problem Statement

"How might we help marketing and HR professionals better understand and respond to the emotional dynamics of their virtual meetings?"

05. DESIGN PROCESS & SOLUTIONS

Balancing Usability with Innovation

Designing for Usability

My first priority was creating a user-friendly landing page. Our research showed that users struggle to accomplish simple tasks on platforms like Zoom because of cluttered interfaces. This wastes valuable time for HR and marketing professionals who need efficiency. I designed our landing page with clear pathways to start or join meetings without distractions.

Leveraging Familiar Design Patterns

Since users are already accustomed to platforms like Google Meet and Zoom, I recognized the importance of working with these established mental models. Rather than forcing users to learn an entirely new system, I designed our video calling interface with familiar elements inspired by Google Meet. This approach reduces the learning curve and helps users feel comfortable immediately.

Biggest Challenge: Effective Data Visualization

The most significant design challenge was presenting emotional data in a meaningful and accessible way. We needed visualizations that anyone could understand at a glance, regardless of their data literacy. My team and I collaborated at the whiteboard, exploring various data representation methods to find the perfect balance between comprehensive insights and clarity.

Beyond Data: Understanding User Needs

While exploring different ways to present emotional data, we realized we needed to dig deeper. Simply showing data wasn't enough—we needed to understand what specific insights would actually help our target audience make better decisions. I conducted additional targeted research with HR and marketing professionals to identify the emotional patterns and indicators that would be most valuable to them in their work.

Respecting Users' Time Constraints

One thing I realized when I went back to doing research is that many users have back-to-back meetings. This means they do not always have the time to view a detailed breakdown of the data. So I designed this pie chart dashboard that pops up once the meeting ends. This helps the user have a quick understanding of the emotions felt throughout the meeting.

Comprehensive Analytics Dashboard

The dashboard was designed to meet varying user needs without difficulty. Users can find any data they want through three presentation methods: a quick pie chart overview for immediate insights, a detailed timeline graph showing emotional flow throughout the meeting, and transcribed highlights where specific emotions spiked. This multi-layered approach accommodates different time constraints and analysis depths.
